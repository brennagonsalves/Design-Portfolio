The Problem

There has been an abrupt shift to a remote service model for rehabilitation therapies since the COVID19 outbreak. The lack of physical office spaces creates some headaches for therapists.

  1. 1. Therapy daily tasks are accomplished on different platforms
  2. 2. Session prep is time consuming, requiring work off the clock
  3. 3. Hard to collaborate with multi-disciplinary team outside of the office space

The Solution

TheraShelf streamlines the most important therapy tasks while promoting collaboration with the multi-disciplinary team. TheraShelf's benefits are three fold, positively impacting everyone involved in the therapy process. Let's take a closer look:

Where do we go from here?

I was able to carve out some actionable insights from the data collected from the surveys and analysis of current cloud storage products. Key takeaways include:

1. Untapped Market

There is a huge potential market for therapists who now work remotely. Design a product to appeal to the new market while solving the major pain points of current cloud storage users.

2. Smart Scheduling

Streamline access to visit schedules, visit types, and client records. These therapy tasks eat up a significant amount of therapist's time. Creating inuitive user flows will improve time management and productivity.

3. Consolidate Therapy Tasks

There are currently no cloud storage products on the market that account for all the most important therapy tasks. Including the right combination of features will separate this product from competitors.

Creating User Personas

I developed personas to represent an inclusive and full spectrum representation of potential TheraShelf users. The personas were referenced frequently during the design process to maintain true user emapthy.

User Stories

I wrote user stories to account for the most important features per survey and SWOT analysis results. These stories were used to create the MVP that would eventually move into usability testing. High priority stories include:

  • I want to view today's schedule
  • I want to add a new visit
  • I want to view client medical records

Sketches

It was finally time to put pen to paper. I started by sketching out possible layouts for the product pages based on the user flows. Rapid iterations allowed me to work through some initial design challenges in a quick and efficient manner.

Wireframes

Using my sketches for reference, I created digital wireframes in Figma. The dashboard serves as an anchor point for product navigation. The user flow was designed to mirror an intuitive therapy process, starting with chart review and ending with visit scheduling.

Usability Testing

Remote usability testing was completed with several participants to assess the effectiveness of collective design decisions up to this point. Let's take a look at some user feedback and subsequent design revisions.

Problem 1: Inefficient dashboard configuration

It took users longer than anticipated to locate high priority features. There were wasteful clicks around the dashboard page before reaching the medical records. The extra time searching through the dashbard hinders therapy productivity.

Iteration 2

Dashboard elements were reconfigured to better communicate hierarchy of information. A hamburger menu replaces the bottom toolbar to use space more effectively and eliminate ambiguous toolbar buttons. A card layout was added to the middle of the page for direct access to high priority features. Color and images were used to draw the eye and add function clarity.

Problem 2: Time consuming diagnostics

It took too long for users to identify the most important diagnostic information from client medical records. The full scale reports are lengthy to read and take up too much space on the medical record screen.

Iteration 2

The medical records summary tab now highlights key diagnostics like standardized tests and diagnoses at a quick glance. The full length version of each assessment may be viewed under a separate tab when therapists have extra time. These revisions improve the communication flow between the multi-disciplinary team.

Problem 3: Slow scheduling process

The schedule page from the first iteration requires a significant amount of free typing. Therapists may also have to navigate back to a different page to double check information like proper spelling of names.

Iteration 2

The entire scheduling process now requires just a few clicks to complete. Free typing information is minimized, saving time and decreasing the chance of errors. It streamlines scheduling by allowing therapist to select only available time slots for their new visit.

Prototyping & Preference Testing

A high fidelity prototype was created in Figma. I was careful to include the visual elements defined by the branding guidlines and revisions from the first round of user testing.

I considered different configurations for the menu on the client overview page. Preference testing was completed to see which visual element was preferred for this product. Users chose between a slide feature or tab menu to highlight content pages during navigation.

The majority of users preferred the slide feature (left image) since it was clearer and more eye catching compared to the tab menu. The design element was implemented throughout the product.

The Final Design

TheraShelf underwent some minor and major changes to its design elements and layout. At the end of the day, TheraShelf finds its place amoungst other cloud storage products by targeting a niche health care audience. It provides creative solutions to our new remote world.
