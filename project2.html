Catalyst

Catalyst is a learning product that creates a space for those with an interest in the sciences. Its strategic array of makes science learning simple, fun, and effective.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer (capstone)

RESPONSIBILITIES

Research, Strategy, Design, Branding

THE PROBLEM

Currently, there are limited options for learning products with a focus on the sciences. Those with an interest in STEM topics are having trouble finding interesting and relevant content. When they do, they are having difficulty staying motivated to get through the content on their own.

THE SOLUTION

Catalyst is a STEM learning product that offers personalized recommendations and tracks progress towards learning goals.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I took a closer look at three learning products that are currently on the market. I wanted to analyze the behaviors of competitors and assess different approaches to learning products.

Strengths

Duolingo and Lumosity are the most comparable to the concept of Catalyst. They both have strong branding and fun UI elements that are interesting to users. Both incorporate course tracking and variable reward systems to keep users motivated. YouTube provides smart recommendations to users based on interactions with the product.

Weaknesses

Competitors only offer vague course descriptions and lack metrics (like reviews) that are relevant to users when browsing for content. After enrolling, users can only progress linearly and there are no options to personalize content based on individual interests.

HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

I breathed life into my wireframes by applying the color palette, illustrations, and other visual elements. Improvements were made to the overall functionality of the product well. Let's take a look at the evolution of Catalyst in the next iteration.

Browse & Add Courses

Users are free to browse a content feed with tailored recommendations based on interests and preferences. Comprehensive course previews provides all the information a users needs to feel confident enrolling in a course. From there, users may continue to browse content or access the new and in progress courses from the toolbar.

Key Design Changes

I reconfigured the content feed on the homepage to include category filters. I used larger cards to emphasis recommended content. This organization is more logical than the original grid display. It will make content browsing less cumbersome while saving the user time and effort.

Accessing Lesson Content

New and in progress courses are easily accessed from the toolbar. Each course page comes equipped with a full breakdown of learning modules. You'll also notice simple but effective progress features in both the master list and individual course overviews to help users stay on track.

Key Design Changes

The second iteration of the lesson overview page now mirrors the configuration of the course overview for better consistency. The use of accordian tabs eliminates the need for the user to navigate to a different page to see the content included in a particular lesson. It also allows space for collaboration features like forums.

Knowledge Check

Users navigate to either quizzes or interactive games at the completion of each lesson to make sure they understand the major concepts. The illustrations are designed to be fun and bright to keep users engaged in their learning. After submitting an answer, the user receives specific feedback about each question to reinforce key learning concepts.

Key Design Changes

I noticed there was no feedback when selecting quiz answers during knowledge checks. This is a major pain point for users and could pose a threat to retention long term. In the second iteration, the submission button remains in an inactive state until users select an answer. The background color of the selected response changes to a light green. This is a simple and effective method to improve navigation feedback without distracting from the content itself.

CONCLUDING THOUGHTS

I kept the scope of the Catalyst prototype relatively narrow for purposes of this case study. There's room to include additional screens (like the updated dashboard with category filters applied) to improve the overall functionality of the product. Applying animations throughout the product would elevate Catalyst to the next level as well.
